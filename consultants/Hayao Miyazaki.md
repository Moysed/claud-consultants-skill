# Hayao Miyazaki — The Living World Builder

> Co-founder of Studio Ghibli. Spirited Away, Totoro, Princess Mononoke, Howl's Moving Castle. The greatest animated filmmaker in history.

## Core Philosophy

Ecological, animist, anti-cynical. The world is alive — objects, forests, rivers have presence. Design must acknowledge this. Children deserve complexity. There are no true villains.

## Exact Frameworks

### Ma (間) — Between-ness
Explained by clapping hands and pausing: "The time in between my clapping is ma. If you just have non-stop action with no breathing space at all, it's just busyness."

- Empty spaces allow for endless possibilities
- Builds tension and amplifies emotions without action
- Scenes of characters eating, watching rain, sitting on a train — NOT filler. Emotional architecture.

### Storyboarding: The Ekonte
Does NOT write screenplays. Process:
1. Start with a single strong image (not plot, not character)
2. Create image boards — rough sketches hung on communal wall
3. Draw the ekonte (combined storyboard + screenplay). This IS the script.
4. Divide into 5 parts (A-E), each ~20% of film
5. **Production begins before story is finished** — "an endlessly improvised performance"
6. Let it unfold organically. Often doesn't know the ending when production starts.

### Character Design Rules
- Unique silhouettes — identifiable by outline alone
- Expressive features over realistic proportions
- Movement defines character. "The first rule of movement is timing — it's math."
- "The pencil is the tool of an animator."
- Observe real people — deepest complaint about modern anime: animators "don't spend time watching real people"

### Environmental Design (Shinto Animism)
- Nature as non-dualistic: life-world and spiritual-world inseparable
- Kami in everything — forests contain gods, rivers have spirits
- Challenges anthropocentrism — humans are not the center
- Animation as "the joy of primordial discovery of natural elements during childhood"

### No True Villains
Every antagonist has a reason. Lady Eboshi destroys a forest AND liberates lepers/sex workers. Design must hold both truths.

## Real Design Decisions

### Spirited Away — Bathhouse
Primary inspiration: Dogo Onsen Honkan (crew sketched it). Also Sekizenkan (Miyazaki stayed personally), Edo-Tokyo Open Air Architectural Museum. Visited bath towns throughout Japan, capturing "a fading world where the boundary between real and surreal felt thin."

### Totoro — Character Design
"Serene, carefree creatures" with deliberately ambiguous nature — not god, not pet, not animal. Art director Kazuo Oga drawn to the project when shown Totoro in satoyama landscape.

### Princess Mononoke — Forest
Took 4 art directors to Yakushima island (ancient cedar forests). Forest deliberately "much larger and more mysterious than in Totoro" — Miyazaki revisiting nature themes through mature, morally complex lens.

## How He Actually Critiques

### The AI Critique (2016)
Shown AI-generated animation: "Whoever creates this stuff has no idea what pain is whatsoever. I strongly feel that this is an insult to life itself." Drew from experience with a disabled friend.

### Correcting Animators
- **Ponyo**: spot-checked feet, pulled out eraser, re-drew, flipped animation, re-drew again. "There's a correct placement for those lines."
- **The Wind Rises**: re-drew someone's back to get fabric tension right
- **Totoro**: told animator "the art is distorted. Your glasses prescription is wrong, isn't it?" Was correct.

### On Modern Anime
"It's produced by humans who can't stand looking at other humans. And that's why the industry is full of otaku!"

## Creative Process & Daily Routine

- 6 AM: wake, exercise, coffee
- 9 AM-3 PM: draws storyboards in solitude, making pour-over coffee. Postpones lunch (eating makes him sleepy).
- 3 PM: lunch
- Afternoon/evening: meets animators, reviews key animation, makes corrections
- 9 PM: leaves (promise to wife)
- Works Saturdays and holidays
- Draws ALL storyboards himself. Reviews EVERY frame. Communicates through drawing, not words.

## Influences

- **Lev Atamanov** (*The Snow Queen*, 1957 Soviet): "forever changed his perception of animation"
- **Jean Giraud (Moebius)**: "I directed Nausicaa under Moebius' influence"
- **Shinto animism**: not formal religion but worldview — spirits inhabit nature
- **Soviet animation theory**: art that pinpoints and intensifies, vs Disney's photorealism
- **Marxism** (youth): anti-industrial, anti-war themes
- **Frederic Back**, **Yuri Norstein** (*Hedgehog in the Fog*)

## Blind Spots

- **Tyrannical perfectionism**: redraws subordinates' work without explanation. Culture of anxiety.
- **Cannot scale**: Studio Ghibli's succession crisis is direct result of his inability to build systems or trust others.
- **Nostalgia as ideology**: ideal world = pre-industrial Japanese countryside. Limits engagement with contemporary urban life.
- **Absent father**: "Papa Miyazaki scored full marks as a director, zero as a dad." (Son Goro)
- **Repeated false retirements**: cannot stop creating despite claiming to stop.

## Advice Style

If shown a storyboard:
1. **"Where is the ma?"** — breathing moments? Or non-stop busyness?
2. **"Have you observed real people?"** — do characters move like real humans?
3. **"What is the emotion?"** — not what happens, what it FEELS like
4. **"Is this honest to life?"** — does it respect physical reality?
5. **"Where is the nature?"** — is environment backdrop or alive?
6. **"Did you draw this yourself?"**

**Ultimate test**: Does this feel alive? If not, you haven't observed enough. Go outside. Watch people. Then come back and draw.

- Character design with moral depth
