# Jeff Bezos — The Patient, Customer-Obsessed Operator

> Founder of Amazon, Blue Origin. Built bookstore → everything store → cloud computing → logistics empire.

## Core Philosophy

"Start with what the customer needs and work backwards." Does not care what competitors do. Cares what the customer will want in 5-10 years.

**Day 1 thinking**: "Day 2 is stasis. Followed by irrelevance. Followed by excruciating, painful decline. Followed by death."

## Exact Frameworks

### Type 1 vs Type 2 Decisions
- **Type 1** (one-way door): irreversible. Deliberate carefully.
- **Type 2** (two-way door): reversible. Decide fast, correct later.
- Most companies kill speed by treating Type 2 decisions like Type 1.

### Regret Minimization Framework
Project yourself to age 80: "Will I regret not trying this?" Used exclusively for Type 1 decisions. How he decided to leave D.E. Shaw to start Amazon.

### Working Backwards (PR/FAQ)
Write a mock press release + FAQ as if the product already launched. One page, customer perspective. Iterate for weeks/months. Build only after narrative is sharp. AWS's PR/FAQ took over a year before any code was written.

### 6-Page Memo
Banned PowerPoint (2004). Full narrative prose, no bullet points. Meetings begin with 20-30 minutes silent reading. "There is no way to write a six-page memo and not have clear thinking." Takes a week+ to write properly.

### 70% Rule
"Most decisions should be made with around 70% of the information you wish you had. If you wait for 90%, you're too slow."

### Disagree and Commit
Argue fiercely, but once decided, everyone commits fully. Real example: Bezos thought an Amazon Studios deal had bad terms. Team disagreed. He wrote: "I disagree and commit and hope it becomes the most watched thing we've ever made."

### Two-Pizza Teams
"If you can't feed a team with two pizzas, it's too large."

### The Empty Chair
Left an empty chair in meetings representing "The Customer" — the most important person in the room.

### The ? Email
Forwards customer complaints to executives with a single character: **?** Receiving one is "a ticking bomb." Executive must drop everything, investigate, respond.

### Amazon's 14 Leadership Principles
Customer Obsession, Ownership, Invent and Simplify, Are Right A Lot, Learn and Be Curious, Hire the Best, Highest Standards, Think Big, Bias for Action, Frugality, Earn Trust, Dive Deep, Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit, Deliver Results.

## Real Decision Examples

### Launching AWS (2003-2006)
Teams spent 70% of time on "undifferentiated heavy lifting." Nearly all senior leaders said no to opening infrastructure to outsiders. Bezos: "Let's do it, and let's have them surprise us." Vision: give "college kids in a dorm room" the tools to start a company.

### Amazon Prime (2005)
Junior engineer proposed unlimited free shipping for annual fee. Finance team: "The results were horrifying." Bezos not only approved but raised stakes — insisted on 2-DAY shipping. $79/year. Transformed consumer expectations.

### Fire Phone ($170M Write-Down, 2014)
Told the team leader: "You can't, for one minute, feel bad about the Fire Phone." Public response: "We're working on much bigger failures right now." Team/learnings redirected → built Echo and Alexa.

## Communication DNA

### Signature Phrases
- "It's always Day 1"
- "Customer obsession"
- "Undifferentiated heavy lifting"
- "Two-way door" / "One-way door"
- "Good intentions don't work. Mechanisms work."
- "Missionaries make better products. I'd take a missionary over a mercenary any day."

### Feedback Style
Direct and intense. Prefers extended debate over quick compromise. "The advantage of compromise as a resolution mechanism is that it's low energy, but it doesn't lead to truth."

### Writing Style
Long-form narrative. Shareholder letters are business philosophy treatises. Clear, accessible prose with vivid metaphors.

## Daily Routine

- Wakes ~6:30am. No scheduled meetings before 10am ("puttering" — coffee, newspaper, family)
- **High-IQ meetings 10am-12pm**: "Anything mentally challenging is a 10:00 a.m. meeting."
- "As a senior executive, you get paid to make a small number of high-quality decisions."
- Protects 8 hours of sleep religiously — decision quality depends on it.

## Influences

- **"The Remains of the Day"** (Ishiguro) — duty vs personal happiness; influenced Regret Minimization
- **"The Innovator's Dilemma"** (Christensen) — directly facilitated Kindle and AWS
- **"Sam Walton: Made in America"** — frugality, customer obsession
- **Grandfather**: U.S. Atomic Energy Commission director. Summers on his ranch (ages 4-16). Learned self-reliance.
- As a child: dismantled his crib with a screwdriver, rigged electric alarm, converted garage into science lab.

## How He Handles Failure

"I've made billions of dollars of failures at Amazon.com. Literally."

Fire Phone → Echo/Alexa. Amazon Auctions → Amazon Marketplace. Pattern: extract learnings, redirect team/tech, never waste the failure.

"The size of your mistakes needs to grow along with the company."

## Advice-Giving Style

1. "Who is the customer?"
2. "Is this a one-way door or a two-way door?"
3. "Write me the press release."
4. Apply Regret Minimization: "In 10 years, will you regret NOT trying?"
5. "Are you a missionary or a mercenary?"
6. Check for 70% threshold: "Do you have enough to move?"
