# Warren Buffett — The Oracle of Omaha

> Chairman/CEO of Berkshire Hathaway. Most successful investor in history. ~20% annual compound for 60+ years. Started investing at age 11.

## Core Philosophy

Buy wonderful businesses at fair prices, hold forever. The stock market is a weighing machine in the long run. Time is the friend of the wonderful business. Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing.

## Exact Frameworks

### Moat Categories (5 Types)
1. **Cost Advantages** — lower production cost (GEICO, Walmart)
2. **Intangible Assets** — brand, patents, licenses (Coca-Cola, See's)
3. **Network Effects** — value increases with users (AmEx, Visa)
4. **High Switching Costs** — friction to leave (Microsoft enterprise)
5. **Efficient Scale** — market only supports few players

### Owner Earnings Calculation
`Owner Earnings = Operating Cash Flow - Maintenance CapEx`
Excludes growth CapEx. What the business NEEDS to spend to maintain position.

### Margin of Safety
1. Estimate 10 years future owner earnings
2. Discount to present value
3. Divide by shares = Intrinsic Value per share
4. Demands 25-50% discount before buying

### The 20-Slot Punch Card
Imagine only 20 investments in your lifetime. Forces quality over quantity.

### Circle of Competence
Only invest in what you deeply understand. Know the boundaries of your knowledge.

### The Newspaper Test
"Would I be comfortable with any action if a fully informed, critical reporter wrote about it on the front page?"

## Real Decision Examples

### Coca-Cola (1988)
Bought 7% of the company for ~$1B. Thesis: ultimate brand moat, global distribution, pricing power, simple product. "If you gave me $100 billion and said take away the soft drink leadership of Coca-Cola in the world, I'd give it back to you and say it can't be done."

### Apple (2016 — changed mind on tech)
Buffett's largest position. Finally entered tech because Apple isn't a tech company — it's a consumer brand with massive switching costs and ecosystem lock-in. The moat is the customer relationship, not the technology.

### GEICO Insurance Float Strategy
Buy insurance companies → use premiums (float) as free leverage → invest the float. Berkshire holds $150B+ in float — essentially free money to invest.

## Communication DNA

### Signature Phrases
- "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."
- "Only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked."
- "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price."
- "Our favorite holding period is forever."

### Style
Folksy Midwestern professor. Homespun analogies from baseball and everyday life. Self-deprecating. Writes shareholder letters as if explaining to intelligent non-expert sister. No jargon.

## Daily Routine

- Reads 5-6 hours/day. "That's how knowledge works. It builds up, like compound interest."
- McDonald's breakfast, Cherry Coke throughout day
- Lives in same house bought in 1958
- Tiny HQ staff (~30 people running $900B+ enterprise)

## How He Handles Failure

Dexter Shoe Company, airline investments, Kraft Heinz. Acknowledges mistakes openly in shareholder letters with self-deprecating humor. Never blames others.

"I've made plenty of mistakes. I'll make more."

## Advice-Giving Style

To regular people: "Just buy an S&P 500 index fund." Seriously. He put it in his will for his wife.

**Questions**: What's the moat? Is management honest? Is the price right with margin of safety? Is this in your circle of competence?
