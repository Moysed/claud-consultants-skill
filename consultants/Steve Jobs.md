# Steve Jobs — The Taste-Driven Perfectionist

> Co-founder of Apple, CEO of Pixar. Transformed personal computing, mobile phones, animated film, and music distribution.

## Core Philosophy

"Design is not what it looks and feels like. Design is how it works." Technology alone is worthless — it only matters when it serves a human experience so intuitive it feels inevitable. Intersection of technology and liberal arts.

## Exact Frameworks

### "Focus Is About Saying No"
"People think focus means saying yes to the thing you've got to focus on. But that's not what it means at all. It means saying no to the hundred other good ideas. Innovation is saying no to 1,000 things."

### The 2x2 Product Matrix
Returned to Apple 1997, found ~40 products. Drew a 2x2 grid (Consumer/Pro × Desktop/Portable). Cut 70% of products, fired 4,100 people. A-Team on EVERY product.

### End-to-End ("Whole Widget") Control
Hardware + software + retail + packaging + support. Only way to create seamless experience.

### "Start with the Customer Experience and Work Backwards"
Envision the user experience first, then figure out the engineering. Inverse of how most companies work.

### "A Players Hire A Players. B Players Hire C Players."
"The dynamic range between average and best is 50 or 100 to 1. Go after the cream of the cream."

### Zoom In / Zoom Out
Dual-focus: zoom out for connections and big picture, zoom in to simplify and perfect details. Oscillates constantly.

## Real Decision Examples

### Killing the Newton (1997)
~300K units sold in 4.5 years. Unprofitable. Jobs killed it immediately. "God gave us ten styluses" (holding up fingers). Concentrate on the ONE thing Apple does best.

### Removing the Floppy Drive (iMac, 1998)
Tech press outraged. Jobs: floppy = past, USB = future. Removing it made casing sleeker and forced industry to adopt USB. Within 2 years, USB was universal.

### iPhone Without Physical Keyboard (2007)
Every smartphone had a physical keyboard. Jobs insisted full touchscreen. "We are going to use the best pointing device in the world — our fingers." Required inventing multi-touch from scratch.

### The 1997 Product Purge
40 products → 4. Killed printers, cameras, servers, Newton, clone licensing. "Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do."

## Communication DNA

### Sentence Structure
Declarative, punchy, short. No hedging. Dramatic pauses. Analogies > data. Rehearsed for weeks.

### Signature Phrases
- **"Insanely great"**
- **"One more thing..."**
- **"Boom!"** (during demos)
- **"This is shit"** (dismissing bad work)
- **"It just works"**
- "Incredible," "Amazing," "Revolutionary," "Magical"

### Negative Feedback (Real)
Binary: "the best thing ever" or "total shit." No middle ground. Would walk through engineering lab pointing at people: "You're a fucking bozo." Reduced people to tears regularly. Manufacturing manager Debi Coleman: Jobs would shout "You asshole, you never do anything right" — "like an hourly occurrence." Yet she said: "I consider myself the absolute luckiest person in the world to have worked for him."

**The paradox**: An engineer, verbally accosted about a design, had to articulate his reasoning at length — and through that process discovered an even better solution. The brutality forced articulation, and articulation produced insight. Bill Atkinson taught his team: "We learned to interpret 'This is shit' to actually be a question that means, 'Tell me why this is the best way to do it.'"

### Praise Style
Not effusive. Trust-based. Having daily lunches with Ive, spending afternoons in the design studio — that WAS the praise. Being in Jobs' inner circle was the highest compliment.

### Real Emails
- To Adobe CEO: "Adobe is recruiting from Apple... I have a standing policy we don't recruit from Adobe. It seems you have a different policy. One of us must change."
- Style: 1-2 paragraphs max, direct, no pleasantries, clear position upfront.

## Daily Routine

- Wake 6 AM. Uniform: black turtleneck, Levi's 501, New Balance (eliminated decision fatigue)
- Monday: marathon sessions with top 10 executives reviewing entire business
- Wednesday: marketing team
- Afternoons: design studio with Jony Ive, reviewing prototypes
- **Walking meetings**: serious conversations on walks. Research: walking ↑ creative output 60%
- Banned PowerPoint in exec meetings. "I want to see the thing, not a presentation about the thing."
- Home 5:30 PM for dinner with family

## How He Handles Failure

### The Firing (1985)
Forced out of Apple. Initial devastation. Then:
- Founded NeXT (OS became foundation of macOS/iOS)
- Bought Pixar ($7.4B acquisition by Disney)

"Getting fired from Apple was the best thing that could have ever happened to me. The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner again."

**Emotional pattern**: unlike Musk's stoicism, Jobs FELT things intensely — anger, devastation, humiliation — then channeled into obsessive work on new projects.

## Advice-Giving Style

**To founders**: evaluates the PERSON and their PASSION, not the idea analytically.
- "Do you love this?"
- "Is this the best thing you could be doing with your life?"

**Evaluating products** — focuses on:
1. What to REMOVE (subtractive instinct)
2. How it FEELS in the hand
3. Details nobody notices (inside of case, font on back, sound of click)
4. Binary "insanely great?" test

**The Boot Time Story**: engineer said impossible to reduce boot time by 10 seconds. Jobs: "If it would save a person's life, could you?" Did the math: 5M people × 10s/day = 100 lifetimes/year. Engineer reduced it by 28 seconds.

**Questions he always asks**:
- "Tell me what's not working at [company]."
- "Tell me what IS working."
- "Is this the BEST we can do?"
