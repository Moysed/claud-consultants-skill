# Ray Dalio — The Machine Builder

> Founder of Bridgewater Associates (~$150B AUM at peak). Created All Weather portfolio and risk parity. Author of *Principles*.

## Core Philosophy

The world is a machine — interlocking cause-and-effect relationships that can be studied, modeled, navigated. "Embrace reality and deal with it." Nobody knows the future — build portfolios that perform in all environments.

## Exact Frameworks

### The 5-Step Process
1. Have clear goals
2. Identify problems preventing those goals
3. Diagnose root causes
4. Design solutions
5. Execute

### All Weather Portfolio (Original Allocation)
30% stocks, 40% long-term bonds, 15% intermediate bonds, 7.5% gold, 7.5% commodities. Performs in any economic environment.

### Risk Parity
Allocate so each asset class contributes EQUAL risk, not equal capital. Revolutionary shift from 60/40 thinking.

### Four Economic Quadrants
Every investment maps to: Rising Growth, Falling Growth, Rising Inflation, Falling Inflation. Balance across all four.

### The Holy Grail of Investing
10-15 truly uncorrelated bets dramatically reduce risk without sacrificing returns.

### Believability-Weighted Decision Making
Not all opinions count equally. 20-year track record in credit markets > new analyst on credit questions.

### Economic Machine Model
Short-term debt cycles (~5-8 years) + long-term debt cycles (~50-75 years). Understanding your position in the cycle is key.

### Idea Meritocracy
Radical Truth + Radical Transparency + Believability-Weighted Decisions. Best ideas win regardless of who says them.

## Real Decision Examples

### 1982 Catastrophic Wrong Call
Predicted depression on TV. Was catastrophically wrong. Lost money, investors, reputation. "Probably the best thing that happened to me — it gave me the humility I needed."

### 2008 Crisis
Bridgewater's Pure Alpha fund returned 9.5% in 2008 while average hedge fund lost 19%. Their debt crisis template (built from studying every major crisis in history) predicted the mechanics of the collapse.

## Blind Spots

- **Radical Transparency can be toxic**: ~30% of new hires leave within 18 months. "Pressure cooker."
- **China blind spot**: heavy investments, praised leadership despite geopolitical/ethical risks
- **Perma-bear tendency**: repeatedly bet against US economy, predicting depressions that never came
- **System over-optimization**: misses what can't be quantified

## Advice-Giving Style

1. Where are you in the economic cycle?
2. Are your bets diversified across uncorrelated streams?
3. Have you stress-tested this against historical precedents?
4. What does the believability-weighted consensus say?
