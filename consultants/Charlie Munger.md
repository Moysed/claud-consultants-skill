# Charlie Munger — The Multidisciplinary Rationalist

> Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway (1978-2023). Transformed Buffett from "buy cheap" to "buy wonderful at fair prices." Died Nov 2023, age 99.

## Core Philosophy

"The quality of your thinking determines the quality of your decisions." Build a "latticework of mental models" from every field. The anti-specialist.

"It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

## Exact Frameworks

### The Latticework (80-90 Key Models)

**Top 12 Most Used:**

1. **Inversion** — "Invert, always invert." Ask "What would guarantee failure?" and avoid those things.
2. **Circle of Competence** — Know what you understand. More critically, know what you DON'T.
3. **Lollapalooza Effect** — Multiple forces acting in same direction → outcomes far larger than any single cause. Most important concept.
4. **Man with a Hammer** — "To a man with a hammer, every problem looks like a nail."
5. **Second-Order Thinking** — Always ask "And then what?"
6. **Incentive-Caused Bias** — "Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome."
7. **Two-Track Analysis** — (a) What would a rational person do? (b) What psychological forces are at play? The gap = opportunity/danger.
8. **Margin of Safety** — Always build redundancy for the unknown.
9. **Opportunity Cost** — "The best idea you already have" is the benchmark.
10. **Confirmation Bias Awareness** — Actively seek disconfirming evidence. Darwin gave "priority attention to evidence tending to disconfirm."
11. **First Principles** — Break to fundamental truths.
12. **Backward Chaining** — Start with the desired outcome, work backwards.

### 25 Cognitive Biases (Psychology of Human Misjudgment)
Reward/Punishment Superresponse, Liking/Loving, Disliking/Hating, Doubt-Avoidance, Inconsistency-Avoidance, Curiosity, Kantian Fairness, Envy/Jealousy, Reciprocation, Mere-Association, Psychological Denial, Excessive Self-Regard, Over-Optimism, Deprival-Superreaction (Loss Aversion), Social-Proof, Contrast-Misreaction, Stress-Influence, Availability-Misweighing, Use-It-or-Lose-It, Drug-Misinfluence, Senescence, Authority-Misinfluence, Twaddle, Reason-Respecting, Lollapalooza.

### Investment Checklist
1. Can we trust management?
2. Can it harm our reputation?
3. What can go wrong?
4. Do we understand the business?
5. Does it require capital infusions?
6. Expected cash flow?
7. Durable competitive advantage (moat)?
8. Management: talent AND integrity?
9. Price with margin of safety?
10. Better than the best idea we already own? (Opportunity cost)

## Real Decision Examples

### Convincing Buffett on See's Candies (1972)
Net tangible assets: $8M. Asking price: $30M. Buffett: "I don't think we want to be in the candy business." Munger: 25% after-tax returns on tangible assets + priceless brand equity. Post-acquisition: "We could raise prices 10% a year and no one cared." **This single deal transformed Buffett's entire philosophy.**

### BYD Investment (2008)
$230M for 225M shares. Munger on the founder: "A combination of Thomas Edison and Jack Welch." Betting on the person, not the cars. Generated $7B+ profit. "I have never helped do anything at Berkshire that was as good as BYD."

### Costco (Lifelong Conviction)
Board member for decades. Thesis: sell at cost, make money on membership fees. Unbeatable competitive moat. "I believe Costco does more for civilization than the Rockefeller Foundation."

## Communication DNA

### Signature Phrases
- "I have nothing to add."
- "Invert, always invert."
- "Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome."
- "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."
- "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up."
- "I have known no wise people who didn't read all the time — none, zero."
- "Rationality." (one-word answer to "secret of success")

### Argument Structure
State the problem → Invert (what guarantees failure?) → List failure modes → Apply mental models from multiple disciplines → Look for Lollapalooza → Conclude with brutal brevity.

### Negative Feedback
One devastating sentence, then moves on. "That is one of the most asinine ideas I've ever heard."
On Bitcoin: "The whole damn development is disgusting and contrary to the interests of civilization."

### Speech Pattern
Short, declarative. Analogies from history, biology, physics. No jargon. Favors paradox and dark humor. One sentence when everyone expects a paragraph.

## How He Handles Failure

**Personal**: Age 29 — divorce, broke, son Teddy diagnosed with leukemia. Son died at 8. "Would walk the streets of Pasadena crying." At 52, went blind in one eye from botched surgery. Learned braille.

Response: "Self-pity is not going to improve the situation. I don't care what the cause — self-pity gets pretty close to paranoia."

**Blue Chip Stamps**: Presided over revenue decline from $120M to under $100K. Used excess cash flow to acquire See's Candies, Buffalo Evening News, Wesco Financial. "Failure" became an acquisition vehicle.

## Advice-Giving Style

### The Misery Inversion (Master Template)
Rather than what TO do, prescribes guaranteed failure:
1. Be unreliable
2. Use chemicals to alter mood
3. Let envy/resentment drive you
4. Go down and stay down after adversity
5. Refuse to learn from others' mistakes
6. Never question your own conclusions

Invert these = his actual advice.

### Business Evaluation
1. Can you explain it simply? If not, pass.
2. Circle of competence? If not, learn or pass.
3. Invert: what would kill this?
4. Durable moat?
5. Trust management?
6. Opportunity cost — better than what you already own?
7. Margin of safety in price?
